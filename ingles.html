    • Biography:

    He was a critical and provocative thinker, known for his literary style and bold philosophy.

    He worked as a professor of classical philology and wrote works such as “Thus Spoke Zarathustra” and “Beyond Good and Evil.”

    His mental health deteriorated in the latter part of his life, and he spent his final years in seclusion.

    Key Ideas:

    Will to Power: Nietzsche believed that the will to power was the driving force behind all human actions. The pursuit of power and affirmation of life were fundamental.

    Übermensch (Superman): He proposed the idea of the “Übermensch,” an individual who transcends traditional norms and values to create their own path.

    Critique of Traditional Morality: Nietzsche questioned Christian morality and considered it a form of mental slavery.

    Eternal Recurrence: He suggested that we would live our lives over and over, requiring us to accept each moment as if it repeated infinitely.
